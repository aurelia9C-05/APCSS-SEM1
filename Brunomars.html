Bruno Mars

Biography

Peter Gene Hernandez, professionally known as Bruno Mars, was born in Honolulu, Hawaii, on October 8th, 1985. His family was quite musical: his father was a percussionist, and his mother was a hula dancer. Burno Mars would begin performing at a very young age, impersonating Elvis Presley in his family's band, The Love Notes. Following high school, he relocated to Los Angeles in 2003 to pursue his music aspirations.

Achievements

Bruno Mars' list of awards and accolades over the years includes the following:

  • Grammy Awards: Best Male Pop Vocal Performance for "Just the Way You Are."
  • American Music Awards: Artist of the Year in 2014.
  • Billboard Music Awards: Top R&B Artist in 2018.

His genres of music include pop, R&B, funk, and soul. He has many hits, such as "Uptown Funk," "That's What I Like," and "Locked Out of Heaven," which have topped charts around the world.

Professional Summary

Bruno Mars is an all-rounder artist, basically a singer, songwriter, record producer, and dancer. He first came into the limelight when he performed as a featured artist with the release of B.o.B's "Nothin' on You" and Travie McCoy's "Billionaire." Doo-Wops & Hooligans, his 2010 debut album, made him an instantly recognizable name and a leading figure in the music world.

Popular Quote

"I'd always been a working musician in Hawaii and never had problems paying rent. And then it's like, 'Now I'm in L.A. and my phone's getting shut off.' That's when reality hit."
