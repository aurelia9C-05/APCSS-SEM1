Tulus

Biography

Muhammad Tulus Rusydi, known simply as Tulus, was born on August 20, 1987, in Bukittinggi, Indonesia. He grew up in a musical environment, with a deep interest in music from a young age, influenced by his mother, who sold music cassettes. Tulus pursued higher education in architecture at Universitas Katolik Parahyangan in Bandung, where he began to explore his musical talents more seriously.

Achievements

Tulus has received over 60 awards in music and cinematography throughout his career. Notable achievements include:

  • 5 AMI Awards for his album Monokrom in 2017.
  • Leading the performance of "Indonesia Raya" at the opening ceremony of the 2018 Asian Games.
  • His album Gajah became the first Indonesian album to enter the Top 10 best-selling albums on iTunes Asia.

Tulus is known for his contributions to Indonesian pop music and continues to be a significant figure in the industry.

Professional Summary

Tulus is a celebrated singer-songwriter and has established his own record label, TulusCompany, to maintain creative control over his work. He began his professional career in 2011 with the release of his debut album titled Tulus, which was well-received and marked a significant milestone in Indonesian music.

Famous Quote

"I decided to be independent with my family's support. I had no fear of not being heard. I believe that there will be space for my music."
